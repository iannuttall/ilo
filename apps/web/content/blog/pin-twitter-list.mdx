Pinned Lists are still one of the better ways to make X (formerly Twitter) usable. You get a focused timeline next to For You and Following, without waiting for the main feed to guess what you meant to read.

The feature is simple, but the current X UI makes it feel more mysterious than it should.

## Pin a List from the Lists screen

X's [Lists help page](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/x-lists) says pinned Lists appear to the right of your For You and Following timelines. The same page describes the control as the pushpin icon in the Lists screen.

The flow is the same on mobile and desktop.

1. Open the profile icon menu.
2. Choose **Lists**.
3. Find a List you created or a List you follow.
4. Tap or click the pushpin icon next to that List.
5. Go back to Home and look beside **For You** and **Following**.

The pinned List should now appear as a timeline tab. On mobile you can tap it or swipe across. On desktop it appears in the Home timeline tab row.

## Unpinning a List does not delete it

Unpinning only removes the shortcut from your Home timeline. It does not delete the List, unfollow the List, or remove any members from a List you own.

To unpin it, go back to **Lists**, look under **Pinned**, and tap or click the highlighted pushpin icon. X's help page describes that as removing the List from your timeline, which is exactly the right mental model. You are removing the shortcut, not the List itself.

This is useful if you want a cleaner Home tab row. Too many pinned Lists turns the top of X into a little filing cabinet. Some people love that. I start forgetting which drawer I meant to open.

## Reorder pinned Lists when one matters most

Pinned Lists can be reordered from the same Lists screen. X says to choose **Edit**, press and hold the reorder icon on the right side of a List, drag it into position, then choose **Done**.

Put the List you use most next to Following. That keeps it one tap or one swipe away on mobile. For creator accounts that might be a customers List, a niche peers List, a journalists List, or a private "read this before posting" List.

Lists work best when they are narrow. A List with 40 carefully chosen accounts beats a List with 900 accounts that recreates the same noisy feed you were trying to escape.

## Lists have practical limits now

Pinned Lists are a reading tool. They are not an analytics tool, a monitoring tool, or a full search surface.

The current limits are worth knowing before you build a workflow around Lists.

- A List timeline only shows posts from accounts in that List.
- You can follow a public List without following the individual accounts in it.
- You do not need to follow an account to add it to one of your own Lists.
- List names are capped at 25 characters and cannot begin with a number.
- To remove yourself from a List someone else created, X says you need to block the List creator.

That last one is the sharp edge. Lists are useful for organizing your own reading, but they are not a perfect consent system for how other people categorize you.

Trying to search inside or around Lists? Pinning will not help much. Use search operators instead, and keep a note of the ones that still work in [ilo's guide to X advanced search](/blog/advanced-twitter-search).
