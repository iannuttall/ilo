X (formerly Twitter) now has a built-in way to remove a follower, so the old soft-block trick is no longer the first move. The catch is annoying in a very X way: the official remove-follower control is still web-only.

Need someone gone from your follower list today? You have three options. Remove them on web, block and unblock them, or block them and leave the block in place.

## Remove this follower is the cleanest option on web

X's own [following FAQ](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/following-faqs) says you can remove a follower from either your followers list or the person's profile page. The same page also says the feature is currently available on web only.

The web flow is short.

1. Open X in a browser.
2. Go to your profile and open your followers list, or open the follower's profile directly.
3. Choose the more menu next to the account.
4. Select **Remove this follower**.
5. Confirm the removal.

That removes the account from your followers. X also makes the tradeoff clear: removing a follower does not stop them from following you again if your posts are public, or requesting to follow you again if your posts are protected.

This is the right option when you want distance without sending the stronger signal of a block. They do not get a "you were removed" notification, but they may notice if they visit your profile and see the Follow button again.

## Soft blocking still works when the remove button is missing

A soft block means you block the account, then unblock it. Blocking removes them as a follower. Unblocking lets them see and follow you again later.

Use this when you are on mobile and cannot find **Remove this follower**, or when X is being X and the menu you need has vanished for no obvious reason.

Soft blocking takes the old route.

1. Open the person's profile.
2. Tap or click the more menu.
3. Choose **Block** and confirm.
4. Open the same menu again.
5. Choose **Unblock** and confirm.

The result is similar to removing a follower. They are no longer following you, but they are not blocked anymore. If your account is public, they can still read your posts and follow you again.

## Blocking makes sense when you need the boundary to stay

Soft blocking is for quiet cleanup. Blocking is for an actual boundary.

X's [blocking help page](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/blocking-and-unblocking-accounts) says blocked accounts cannot follow you, DM you, engage with your posts, add you to Lists, or tag you in photos. It also says public posts can still be seen by blocked accounts, though they cannot engage with them.

That last part matters. A block limits interaction inside X. It is not a privacy wall for a public account.

Leave the block in place when the account is harassing you, repeatedly refollowing, scraping your replies, adding you to unwanted Lists, or trying to contact you through DMs. The social cost is higher, because someone can tell they are blocked if they visit your profile. Sometimes that is the point.

## Protected posts are the only way to approve future followers

Removing one follower does not change who can follow you next. Protecting your posts does.

X's [protected posts help page](https://help.x.com/en/safety-and-security/public-and-protected-posts) says public posts are visible to anyone, whether or not they have an X account. Protected posts are visible only to your followers, and new followers have to send a request you can approve or deny.

The tradeoffs are still there.

- Existing followers can keep seeing protected posts unless you remove or block them.
- Followers can still screenshot or share what they see.
- Links to media can still be accessed by anyone who has the media URL.
- If you unprotect your posts later, previously protected posts become public.

So the privacy stack is pretty blunt. Remove a follower when you want cleanup. Soft block when mobile gets in the way. Block when the person should stay away. Protect posts when you want approval over future followers.
